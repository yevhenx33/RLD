RLD stands for Rate-Level Derivatives. The protocol treats a borrow rate as the underlying asset of a derivative market. Instead of tracking ETH/USD or BTC/USD, an RLD market tracks the annualized borrow rate of a lending pool such as Aave USDC or Morpho USDC.

## The Primitive

Each market defines an index price:

```text
P_index(t) = K * r_t
```

`r_t` is the observed borrow rate and `K` is the scalar. The paper and Aave oracle implementation use `K = 100`, so a 5% borrow rate becomes a 5.00 price, 10% becomes 10.00, and 100% becomes 100.00.

The tradeable token is `wRLP`, a fungible ERC-20 debt token minted by shorts and bought by longs.

## Actors

| Actor | What they do | Economic exposure |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Long | Buys `wRLP` on the pool | Benefits when rates rise. |
| Short / minter | Deposits collateral, mints `wRLP`, usually sells it | Benefits when rates fall or when funding reduces debt. |
| LP | Provides liquidity in the `wRLP` / collateral pool | Earns trading fees and absorbs inventory risk. |
| Bond user | Opens a short and pairs it with a timed TWAMM order | Converts floating yield into fixed-duration yield. |
| Borrower hedger | Buys `wRLP` against variable-rate debt | Offsets higher borrowing costs. |
| CDS buyer | Holds protection that appreciates in pool-stress events | Receives parametric payout from rate/utilization spike. |
| CDS underwriter | Shorts protection against uncorrelated collateral | Earns premium but risks collateral seizure at settlement. |

## Long Exposure

A long does not mint. They buy `wRLP` from the market.

If the index moves from 5.00 to 10.00, a long who holds 200 `wRLP` has a 1,000 unit mark-to-index gain before fees and funding effects:

```text
PnL = Q * (P_1 - P_0)
PnL = 200 * (10 - 5) = 1,000
```

This is useful for borrowers, fintech lenders, and traders who need protection from rising variable rates.

## Short Exposure

A short mints `wRLP` through a broker-backed CDP position:

1. The user creates or owns a `PrimeBroker`.
2. Collateral is deposited into the broker.
3. The broker enters `RLDCore.lock()`.
4. During the lock, `RLDCore.modifyPosition()` increases debt principal.
5. `PositionToken.mint()` mints `wRLP` to the broker.
6. The broker or router can sell the `wRLP` into the v4 pool.

The short owes debt principal scaled by the market normalization factor:

```text
trueDebt = debtPrincipal * normalizationFactor
debtValue = trueDebt * P_index
```

Shorts are useful for fixed-yield products, rate mean-reversion trades, liquidity provisioning, and CDS underwriting.

## Funding

RLD avoids explicit hourly cash funding. Instead, it updates each market's `normalizationFactor` lazily during state-changing interactions.

For standard markets, funding is based on mark-index divergence:

```text
fundingRate = (normalizedMark - index) / index
NF_new = NF_old * exp(-fundingRate * dt / fundingPeriod)
```

When the market trades above the index, shorts are rewarded through a lower debt scaler. When the market trades below the index, shorts are penalized through a higher debt scaler. The implementation uses exponential compounding rather than the paper's linearized notation.

CDS markets can use a separate absolute decay model:

```text
NF_new = NF_old * exp(-F * dt / secondsPerYear)
```

This model is independent of spot price and uses the per-market `decayRateWad`.

## Duration

The base `wRLP` token is perpetual. Duration is created outside the token by pairing positions with TWAMM execution.

For a fixed-duration hedge, the intended notional decays linearly:

```text
Q(t) = Q_initial * (1 - elapsed / duration)
```

This is the core bridge between a perpetual rate asset and fixed-income products. The token stays perpetual; the account, bond factory, or strategy schedules the unwind.

## Three Product Layers

RLD intentionally uses one liquidity primitive for multiple products:

| Layer | Product | Key mechanism |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Base | Rate-level perp | `wRLP` trades against collateral in a v4/Ghost pool. |
| Structured | Synthetic bond and fixed borrow | Broker plus TWAMM duration matching. |
| Insurance | CDS | Isolated collateral, withdrawal delay, settlement, and collateral seizure. |

The result is a unified rate-liquidity layer instead of many dated markets with fragmented depth.
